What we'd ideally like is to get <mrow><mo>-</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow> on negation.

When do we get negation? When the negative is

  • At the start of the whole expression, or start of expression inside brackets
  • after list separator
  • after equality, comparison, or other operators like cup, oplus, etc
  • in something like sin -3 or vec -3 (probably? currently func and mover only applies to -)
  • after * or /, maybe after - (like 3 - -4)
  • at the start sup/sub after ^ or _

This doesn't seem to fit well with the parsing grammar, since expression-breaking operators like list, equality, etc. aren't parsed specially. To hack it on, we'd have to track "start of expression" somehow. Nothing in the current symbol records is sufficient for that.

Could maybe treat all negatives as negation except those that follow mn, mi, mtext, msqrt, or mrow tags. Could maybe do inside Sexpr, if called with previous term tag type.
